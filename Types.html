"PROSE"

- it refers to any writing that follows the conventions of speech and language. Prose is a communicative style that sounds natural and uses grammatical structure. Prose is the opposite of verse, or poetry, which employs a rhythmic structure that does not mimic ordinary speech.

"POETRY"

- Poetry is a type of literary work that uses language to express emotions, ideas, experiences, or tell a story in a unique and imaginative way. Poetry often employs techniques such as rhyme, meter, and metaphor to create a musical quality and to enhance its meaning. It can take many forms, such as sonnets, haikus, free verse, and others.

There are three (3) types of Poetry. These include the following:

I. NARRATIVE POETRY

- this form describes important events in life either real or imaginary.

III. DRAMATIC POETRY

- is a type of poetry that is meant to be performed on stage or read aloud, usually with the intention of conveying a specific message or telling a story. Dramatic poetry often includes dialogue between characters and is written in a style that is intended to be both emotional and engaging.

TYPES DESCRIPTION
1.Comedy

The word comedy comes from the Greek term “komos” meaning festivity or revelry. This form usually is light and written with the purpose of amusing, and usually has a happy ending.
2.Melodrama

a type of dramatic performance that is characterized by exaggerated emotions, sentimental and often over-the-top characters, and a clear distinction between good and evil.
3.Tragedy

This involves the hero struggling mightily against dynamic forces; he meets death or ruin without success and satisfaction obtained by the protagonist in a comedy.

4.Farce

This is an exaggerated comedy. It seeks to arouse mirth by laughable lines; situations are too ridiculous to be true; the characters seem to be caricatures and the motives undignified and absurd.

5.Social Poems

This form is either purely comic or tragic and it pictures the life of today. It may aim to bring about changes in the social conditions.
